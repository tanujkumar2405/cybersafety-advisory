Our 5-Step Advisory Process

A calm, structured approach to help you navigate cyber fraud incidents safely and confidently

1

Calm & Assess

Pause further risk and collect details safely.

Take a breath. Stop all communication with the suspected fraudster. Do not perform actions under pressure. Create a calm environment and gather incident details:

  • When did this start?
  • How did the contact occur (phone, email, message)?
  • What information have you shared?
  • Is there ongoing risk?
2

Classify

Understand incident severity and urgency.

Every incident is different. We help you understand your situation:

  • Active fraud: Money transfers or immediate pressure
  • Information loss: Sensitive data shared
  • Attempted fraud: Detected before loss
  • Suspicion: Something feels wrong
3

Guide Actions

Clear, actionable next steps for your situation.

Based on your case, we provide practical guidance:

  • Active transfer: Contact bank immediately
  • Compromised credentials: Change passwords, enable 2FA
  • Data shared: Monitor accounts closely
  • Preserve evidence: Screenshots and timestamps
4

Reporting

Navigate official channels for official action.

Official channels handle real recovery. We guide you:

  • Banks: Fraudulent transactions
  • Police: Cybercrime units
  • Telecom: Fraudulent calls/SMS
  • Platforms: Fake profiles
5

Prevention

Build digital safety habits for the future.

Prevention is as important as response:

  • Never share OTPs or passwords
  • Verify through official channels
  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Enable 2-factor authentication

Our 5-Step Advisory Process

A calm, structured approach to help you navigate cyber fraud incidents safely

1

Calm & Assess

Pause further risk and collect details safely.

The first step is to take a breath. Stop all communication with the suspected fraudster. Do not perform any actions under pressure. Create a calm environment to assess what has happened. Gather details about the incident:

  • When did this start?
  • How did the contact occur (phone, email, message)?
  • What information have you shared (if any)?
  • Is there ongoing risk (active transfer, active contact)?
2

Classify the Incident

We help you determine urgency and next steps.

Every incident is different. We help you understand the severity of your situation:

  • Active fraud: Money is being transferred, or you're under immediate pressure
  • Information loss: You've shared sensitive data (OTP, password, account details)
  • Attempted fraud: You detected the scam before any loss occurred
  • Suspicion: Something feels wrong, and you need clarification

This helps us give you advice tailored to your specific situation.

3

Guide Safe Actions

Practical steps you can take right now.

Based on your situation, we provide clear, actionable steps:

  • If money is being transferred: Contact your bank immediately
  • If credentials are compromised: Change passwords and enable 2FA
  • If personal data was shared: Monitor accounts for suspicious activity
  • Document everything: Keep screenshots, messages, and timestamps
  • Disconnect from the fraudster: Block numbers, emails, and accounts

We do not take action for you, but we empower you to protect yourself.

4

Reporting Awareness

How to report to official channels when needed.

Official channels are critical for real action. We guide you on reporting:

  • Banks: Report fraudulent transactions immediately for potential reversal
  • Law enforcement: File a report with police cybercrime units (for larger losses)
  • Telecom providers: Report fraudulent calls and SMS used in the scam
  • Online platforms: Report fake profiles and phishing links
  • Government resources: Use official portals for cyber crime reporting

We help you understand which channels are relevant to your situation.

5

Future Safety

Practical advice to reduce recurrence.

Prevention is as important as response. We share strategies to stay safe:

  • Never share OTPs, passwords, or CVV codes with anyone
  • Verify contacts through official channels before acting
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account
  • Enable 2-factor authentication on critical accounts
  • Be skeptical of urgent requests from unknown sources
  • Educate family members about common scams

Small habits create strong digital hygiene over time.

What We Do

  • Explain reporting and recovery options
  • Offer emotional support through stressful situations
  • Educate on cyber safety and prevention

What We Do NOT Do

  • Ask for OTPs, passwords, or sensitive information
  • Access or recover your accounts
  • Handle or recover money
  • Guarantee specific outcomes
  • Act on behalf of banks or police
  • Make demands or threats
