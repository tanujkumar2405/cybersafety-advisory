Our 4-Step Advisory Process

A calm, structured approach to help you navigate cyber fraud incidents safely and confidently

Step 1: First Contact

When you reach out, we start by understanding your situation calmly and checking if the incident is ongoing.

What happens:

  • We listen without judgment to understand what happened
  • We assess if there is ongoing risk that needs immediate attention
  • Our goal is to pause further harm and create a calm environment

Why this matters: Many fraud victims feel panic and pressure. We help you pause, breathe, and think clearly before taking any action.

Step 2: Guidance & Awareness

We explain the scam type in simple language and help you understand what happened.

What we do:

  • Explain the type of scam in plain, non-technical language
  • Help you understand what information may have been compromised
  • Explain common next steps fraudsters might try
  • Reassure you that you are not alone ‚Äî fraud affects many people

Important: You remain in control of all your accounts and decisions. We advise; you decide what to do.

Step 3: Reporting Support

We guide you to official channels and show you how to file reports with the right authorities.

Official channels we guide you to:

  • Your bank or financial institution ‚Äî if money was transferred
  • Government cybercrime portals ‚Äî to file formal complaints
  • Local law enforcement ‚Äî if there are criminal elements
  • Platform support ‚Äî if social media or email was compromised

Important: You file these reports yourself. We guide you through the process, but you maintain control and direct contact with authorities.

Step 4: Prevention & Future Safety

We share practical safety guidance to help reduce your risk of becoming a victim again.

What we provide:

  • A practical digital safety checklist you can download and use
  • Common red flags to watch for in the future
  • Cyber hygiene best practices tailored to your situation
  • Ongoing resources and educational materials

Education-first support builds long-term safety. Understanding how fraud works is your best defense.
