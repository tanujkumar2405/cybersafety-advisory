Safety Resources & Guidance

Practical checklists, tips, and tools to stay safe online

If You're Facing Active Fraud Right Now

  1. Stop communicating with the fraudster immediately
  2. Do not share any OTPs, passwords, or account details
  3. Contact your bank immediately if money is involved ‚Äî they may reverse transfers
  4. Disconnect from the internet if you fear device compromise
  5. Change passwords on critical accounts (email, banking, social media)
  6. Enable 2FA on all accounts that support it
  7. Monitor accounts for suspicious transactions
  8. File a complaint with local law enforcement (for cyber crimes)

Time is critical in active fraud situations. Official channels (banks, police) must handle the incident, not advisory services like ours.

Cyber Hygiene Best Practices

üîê Password Security

  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Make passwords at least 12 characters long
  • Include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Never write passwords on paper or share them
  • Use a password manager if possible
  • Change passwords immediately if compromised

üì± Mobile Security

  • Enable 2-factor authentication (2FA) on all accounts
  • Keep your phone's OS and apps updated
  • Download apps only from official stores
  • Disable auto-connect to public Wi-Fi
  • Use a VPN on public networks
  • Enable location services selectively

üõ°Ô∏è Device Safety

  • Install and maintain antivirus software
  • Update your device regularly
  • Back up important files regularly
  • Review installed apps periodically
  • Disable Bluetooth when not in use
  • Use a firewall on your computer

üíª Banking Safety

  • Never share your OTP with anyone
  • Verify bank requests through official numbers
  • Use official banking apps, not web browsers
  • Review transaction statements regularly
  • Enable transaction alerts on your account
  • Report suspicious activity immediately

üìß Email Protection

  • Use a strong, unique email password
  • Enable 2FA on your email account
  • Review connected apps periodically
  • Be cautious of email attachments
  • Verify sender addresses before clicking links
  • Report phishing emails to your provider

üåê Social Media Safety

  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Enable privacy settings (make profile private)
  • Limit what information you share publicly
  • Be cautious of friend requests from strangers
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links
  • Report fake profiles or scams immediately

UPI & Digital Payment Safety

UPI Safety

  • Never share your UPI PIN with anyone
  • Be cautious of quick payment requests
  • Verify merchant details before paying
  • Enable transaction limits on UPI apps
  • Review transaction history regularly
  • Use official UPI apps from banks

Credit & Debit Card Safety

  • Never share your CVV or card details online
  • Cover the CVV when swiping
  • Monitor card statements monthly
  • Report lost or stolen cards immediately
  • Shred receipts with card information
  • Use EMI/card lock features

Social Media Account Protection

Steps to Protect Your Social Media

  1. Use a strong, unique password for each social media account
  2. Enable 2-factor authentication on all platforms
  3. Review connected apps and remove ones you no longer use
  4. Set your profile to private and limit who can see your posts
  5. Be selective about friend requests from strangers
  6. Verify URLs before logging in (e.g., facebook.com vs faceb00k.com)
  7. Never click suspicious links in messages or comments
  8. Report fake profiles and scams immediately
  9. Avoid sharing personal information like phone number, address, or date of birth
  10. Use official apps from app stores, not third-party versions
